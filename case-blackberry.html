Blackberry

Blackberry Smartphones India

Blackberry was looking at re-entering the Indian Market with Blackberry EVOLVE, a mid-range Android Smartphone and approached us to design a clutter-breaking campaign to put it back on the map.

Blackberry was known back in its hey-days for its slick-black phones with industry-leading business apps and security features. But that was pre smartphone era. Security was something that set it apart, used by some of the world‚Äôs most powerful leaders. This feature could help the brand make a comeback on its core-strengths in an otherwise feature driven mid-range mobile phone market. Also around this time the Cambridge Analytica Privacy Scandal news had been breaking loose and the launch were to be around the time we reckoned the scandal would explode. So the topical environment worked perfectly as well.

The question then was: do the evolved Millennials, our broad target audience, still consider security an important factor in the buying decision? So we decided to step-out and ask them.

We found that while people very much want security, the perspective has shifted. The concern is no more about their work and mails, it has gotten more personal. People want protection against losing their photos, messages and other personal details. They are afraid of their privacy being violated. Privacy is the new security! But these folks are also not a lot to compromise on features either, they want their phone to do all the things most phone do. They want their phone to be their closest friend and confidant.

We figured the our best approach is to appeal to the heart and the mind both, while the heart wants to do more, the mind needs to rest assured, we please the heart with our features and the mind with safety, our strength and do so playfully.

The campaign was designed in a phased manner to build a unified narrative over a long period. Starting with establishing the need for privacy, introducing the phone, establishing its functional features and building conversations.
